Indian Government Schemes Supporting Startups (30 Schemes)
1. Startup India Initiative
The Startup India Initiative was launched by the Government of India in 2016 to promote innovation and entrepreneurship across the country. The scheme aims to create a strong startup ecosystem that supports economic growth and job creation. Eligible startups receive benefits such as tax exemptions for three consecutive years, simplified regulatory processes, and self-certification under selected labor and environmental laws. The initiative also offers fast-track patent examination and reduced intellectual property fees. Additionally, the government created a Fund of Funds with a corpus of ₹10,000 crore to support startups through venture capital investments.
2. Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)
Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana was launched in 2015 to provide financial assistance to micro and small businesses that require funding to start or expand their operations. The scheme provides loans of up to ₹10 lakh through banks and financial institutions. The loans are categorized into three types: Shishu (up to ₹50,000), Kishor (₹50,000 to ₹5 lakh), and Tarun (₹5 lakh to ₹10 lakh). These loans are usually provided without collateral, helping entrepreneurs who lack assets to secure traditional loans.
3. Atal Innovation Mission (AIM)
Atal Innovation Mission promotes innovation and entrepreneurship by establishing innovation labs and incubation centers. It supports startups through Atal Incubation Centres that provide infrastructure, mentorship, and funding opportunities. Grants of up to ₹10 crore are provided to establish incubators that support entrepreneurs in transforming innovative ideas into viable businesses.
4. SIDBI Fund of Funds for Startups
The Fund of Funds for Startups is managed by the Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI). Instead of directly investing in startups, the fund invests in SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds that further invest in promising startups. The scheme has a corpus of ₹10,000 crore and aims to encourage private investment in early-stage startups.
5. Digital India Initiative
Digital India aims to transform India into a digitally empowered society. It supports technology startups by improving digital infrastructure, encouraging digital services, and providing access to government digital platforms and cloud services. The initiative promotes digital entrepreneurship and supports innovation in technology sectors.
6. Stand-Up India Scheme
The Stand-Up India Scheme encourages entrepreneurship among women and members of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes. The scheme provides bank loans ranging from ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore to start businesses in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors. Each bank branch is expected to support at least one woman entrepreneur and one SC/ST entrepreneur.
7. Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE)
CGTMSE helps startups and small businesses obtain loans without collateral. Under this scheme, the government guarantees a portion of the loan amount provided by banks. This reduces risk for lenders and makes it easier for new entrepreneurs to access credit facilities. The scheme supports loans up to ₹2 crore.
8. ASPIRE Scheme
The ASPIRE scheme promotes innovation and entrepreneurship in rural areas. It supports the creation of incubation centers and livelihood business incubators that provide training and financial support to entrepreneurs. The scheme focuses on agriculture-based industries and rural enterprises.
9. Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC)
BIRAC supports biotechnology startups by providing funding, research support, and mentorship. Programs such as the Biotechnology Ignition Grant offer financial assistance of up to ₹50 lakh for early-stage biotech startups to develop innovative healthcare and agricultural solutions.
10. SAMRIDH Scheme
SAMRIDH supports technology startups through accelerators by providing funding and mentorship. Startups can receive funding support of up to ₹40 lakh along with guidance from industry experts. The scheme aims to help startups scale their products and reach markets successfully.
11. National Initiative for Developing and Harnessing Innovations (NIDHI)
NIDHI supports technology-driven startups by providing incubation support, seed funding, and mentoring. The program helps innovators transform ideas into market-ready products and strengthen the innovation ecosystem in India.
12. Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)
The Startup India Seed Fund Scheme provides financial support to early-stage startups for prototype development, product testing, and market entry. Startups can receive up to ₹20 lakh for prototype development and up to ₹50 lakh for commercialization through government-recognized incubators.
13. Multiplier Grants Scheme (MGS)
The Multiplier Grants Scheme encourages collaboration between academic institutions and private industries in research and development. The government matches funding provided by private companies for technology development projects carried out with research institutions.
14. TIDE 2.0 Scheme
Technology Incubation and Development of Entrepreneurs (TIDE 2.0) supports startups working in emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence, blockchain, and the Internet of Things. The scheme provides grants, incubation facilities, and mentorship support.
15. MSME Innovative Scheme
This scheme promotes innovation, product design, and intellectual property development among micro, small, and medium enterprises. It provides financial assistance for product design improvements, prototype development, and patent registration.
16. Startup India Credit Guarantee Scheme
This scheme improves access to credit for startups by providing government-backed guarantees against loans issued by financial institutions. The program reduces lending risk and encourages banks to finance early-stage startups.
17. Design Clinic Scheme for MSMEs
The Design Clinic Scheme helps MSMEs improve product design and innovation by providing expert consultation and financial support. The initiative helps businesses develop competitive products and enhance market value.
18. Support for International Patent Protection in Electronics and IT (SIP-EIT)
SIP-EIT assists startups and MSMEs in filing international patents by covering a portion of the costs involved. The scheme helps Indian companies protect their intellectual property globally.
19. Software Technology Parks of India (STPI)
STPI provides infrastructure, incubation facilities, and high-speed internet connectivity to IT startups. The initiative also promotes export opportunities for Indian software companies and supports technology entrepreneurs.
20. Electronics Development Fund (EDF)
The Electronics Development Fund supports startups in electronics and semiconductor sectors. It invests in venture capital funds that finance innovative companies working in electronics manufacturing and chip design.
21. National SC/ST Hub
The National SC/ST Hub promotes entrepreneurship among Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes by providing training, financial support, and market access opportunities.
22. Coir Udyami Yojana
This scheme supports entrepreneurs involved in the coir industry by providing financial assistance for setting up coir manufacturing units and purchasing machinery.
23. Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme
The scheme promotes self-employment in the dairy sector by providing financial support for dairy farms, milk processing units, and cold storage infrastructure.
24. National Agriculture Market (e-NAM)
e-NAM is an online trading platform that connects agricultural markets across India, enabling farmers and agribusiness startups to trade agricultural commodities digitally.
25. Technology Development Board (TDB) Funding Scheme
The Technology Development Board provides financial assistance to companies developing innovative technologies. The scheme supports commercialization of research-based technologies.
26. Market Development Assistance (MDA) Scheme
The Market Development Assistance Scheme provides financial support for startups and MSMEs to participate in trade fairs and exhibitions, helping them expand market reach.
27. National Small Industries Corporation (NSIC) Support Scheme
NSIC supports small enterprises through marketing assistance, financial support, raw material procurement support, and training programs.
28. Micro and Small Enterprises Cluster Development Programme (MSE-CDP)
This scheme promotes the development of industrial clusters to improve productivity and competitiveness among small businesses by providing infrastructure and shared resources.
29. National Manufacturing Competitiveness Programme (NMCP)
NMCP focuses on improving manufacturing capabilities of MSMEs by supporting technology upgrades, lean manufacturing practices, and quality improvement initiatives.
30. Sustainable Finance Scheme for MSMEs
This scheme promotes environmentally sustainable business practices by providing financial incentives to companies adopting eco-friendly technologies and sustainable production processes.
