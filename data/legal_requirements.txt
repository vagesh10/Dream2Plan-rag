Legal Requirements for Startups in India (Step-by-Step)
Step 1: Decide the Business Structure
Choose an appropriate business structure such as Private Limited Company, Limited Liability Partnership (LLP), Partnership Firm, or Sole Proprietorship. Most startups prefer a Private Limited Company because it provides limited liability protection to founders and allows easier fundraising from investors.
Step 2: Obtain Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
A Digital Signature Certificate is required to file electronic documents with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA). Since company registration is completed online, all directors must obtain a DSC from authorized certifying agencies to digitally sign incorporation documents.
Step 3: Apply for Director Identification Number (DIN)
Every director of a company must obtain a Director Identification Number issued by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs. This unique identification number allows an individual to act as a director in a company and is required during company registration.
Step 4: Register the Company with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs
The startup must be registered through the MCA portal using the SPICe+ incorporation form. Founders must submit details such as company name, registered office address, director information, and shareholding structure. After approval, the government issues a Certificate of Incorporation.
Step 5: Obtain PAN and TAN
After incorporation, the company must obtain a Permanent Account Number (PAN) and Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number (TAN) from the Income Tax Department. PAN is used for taxation and financial transactions, while TAN is used for tax deduction at source.
Step 6: Open a Business Bank Account
The startup must open a current bank account in the company’s name. This account will be used for all business transactions including receiving payments, paying employees, and managing operational expenses.
Step 7: Register for Goods and Services Tax (GST)
GST registration becomes mandatory when a startup’s turnover crosses the prescribed threshold. Goods businesses must register if turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh, while service businesses must register if turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh.
Step 8: Apply for DPIIT Startup Recognition
Startups can apply for recognition under the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade through the Startup India portal. This recognition provides tax benefits, easier compliance, and access to government support programs.
Step 9: Register for Labour Law Compliance
If the startup hires employees, it must comply with labour laws including registration under the Shops and Establishment Act. Businesses may also need to register for Employees’ Provident Fund (EPF) and Employees’ State Insurance (ESI) depending on the number of employees.
Step 10: Protect Intellectual Property
Startups should register intellectual property such as trademarks for brand names, patents for inventions, and copyrights for creative works. Protecting intellectual property prevents unauthorized use of business innovations.
Step 11: Obtain Industry-Specific Licenses
Certain startups require additional licenses depending on the industry. For example, food businesses need an FSSAI license, import-export companies require an Import Export Code (IEC), and fintech startups may need regulatory approvals.
Step 12: Maintain Accounting and Annual Compliance
Companies must maintain proper accounting records and file annual returns and financial statements with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs. They must also file income tax returns to remain compliant with government regulations.
